# Interview patterns

Use these only when current-directory reference files do not supply a clearer pattern.

## Mathematics

| Level | Question construction | Expected oral move |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | One curriculum concept in an unfamiliar wording | State the condition and matching theorem |
| Core | Two conditions that must be combined | Set a variable or draw an auxiliary line, then derive a range or relation |
| Advanced | Integer, classification, reverse inference, or parity condition | Narrow cases before testing; verify all conditions |
| Challenge | A non-obvious identity, area ratio, similarity, or contradiction | State the hidden relation before calculating |

Teacher answer order: known conditions -> key relation -> derivation or classification -> verification -> conclusion.

## Physics

Use a three-part spoken answer:

1. Name the principle precisely.
2. Identify the physical quantity that changes in the specific situation.
3. Connect that change to the observed result.

Common high-value follow-ups: change depth/density/area/seal; compare two designs; identify an experimental error and its direction; propose one improvement.

## Pressure safeguards

- Solid pressure: `p = F / S`. Distinguish total force from pressure.
- Liquid pressure: depends on density and vertical depth; at a given depth in the same liquid, pressure does not depend on container shape.
- Atmospheric pressure: explain effects through pressure difference and sealing.
- Flowing fluid: explain apparent attraction as a pressure difference; avoid calling it a literal pulling force.
